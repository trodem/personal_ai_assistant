# Terms of Service Baseline (MVP)

## Scope
These baseline terms apply to the MVP of Personal AI Assistant and define minimum user and operator obligations before production launch.

## Service Description
- The service provides personal memory capture, storage, retrieval, and assistant responses based on user-provided data.
- The service is not intended as legal, medical, tax, or financial advice.

## Account and Access
- Users are responsible for account credentials and any activity under their account.
- Access may be suspended or terminated for abuse, fraud, policy violations, or security risk.
- Role-based access controls apply to privileged product areas.

## Acceptable Use
- Users must not upload illegal, harmful, or malicious content.
- Users must not attempt unauthorized access, data exfiltration, or service disruption.
- Automated abuse, scraping beyond policy, and credential sharing are prohibited.

## User Content
- Users retain ownership of their content.
- By using the service, users grant permission to process content for product functionality (capture, extraction, retrieval, analytics/operations).
- Users are responsible for ensuring they have rights to uploaded content.

## AI and Output Limitations
- AI outputs may be incomplete or incorrect and must be reviewed by users before critical decisions.
- Memory persistence follows explicit confirmation flows where required by product policy.

## Billing and Plans (MVP Baseline)
- Plan behavior follows documented product policy (`free`/`premium`, role-based constraints).
- Billing operations may depend on third-party payment providers.
- The operator may change plan features/limits with prior notice where feasible.

## Availability and Changes
- MVP service availability is best effort; interruptions and maintenance may occur.
- Features, limits, and integrations may change during MVP evolution.

## Data Export and Deletion
- Users can request data export and account/data deletion according to product lifecycle policy.
- Deletion workflows are asynchronous and may require processing time.

## Liability Baseline
- To the maximum extent permitted by law, the service is provided "as is" without warranties.
- The operator is not liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages from service use.

## Governing and Contact Baseline
- Governing-law and jurisdiction clauses must be finalized before public launch.
- A support/legal contact must be defined before production release.

## Revision Policy
- This baseline is a pre-launch draft and must be reviewed with legal counsel before public availability.
